Free energy of ideal gas

The Hamiltonian of $N$ ideal-gas (non-interacting) particle is $$ \mathcal{H}(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{p}) = \sum_{i=1}^{3N} \dfrac{p_i^2}{2m_i}, $$ where $\mathbf{q}$ and $\mathbf{p}$ are the generalized coordinates and momenta, and $m_i$ is the mass the $i$-th particle. The partition function is $$ Z(N,V,T) = \frac{1}{N! h^{3N}} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \text{d}\mathbf{q}\text{d}\mathbf{p}\, \text{exp}\left[-\beta \mathcal{H}(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{p})\right] = \frac{V^N}{N! h^{3N}} \int \text{d}\mathbf{p} \, \exp\left(-\beta \sum_{i=1}^{3N} \frac{p_i^2}{2m_i}\right) = \frac{V^N}{N! h^{3N}} \prod_{i=1}^{3N} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \text{d}p_i \, \exp\left(-\beta \frac{p_i^2}{2m_i}\right), \tag{1}\label{1} $$ where $\beta \equiv 1/(k_\text{B}T)$. Consider the integral: $$ \int_{-\infty}^\infty \text{d} p_i \, \exp\left(-\beta \frac{p_i^2}{2m_i}\right) \xrightarrow{x\equiv p_i\sqrt{\beta/2m_i}} \sqrt{\frac{2m_i}{\beta}} \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-x^2} \,\text{d} x = \sqrt{\frac{2\pi m_i}{\beta}}. \tag{2}\label{2} $$ Applying Eq. \eqref{2} in Eq. \eqref{1}, $$ Z(N,V,T) = \frac{V^N}{N!h^{3N}} \prod_{i=1}^{3N} \sqrt{\frac{2\pi m_i}{\beta}} = \frac{V^N}{N!} \left( \frac{2\pi\bar{m}k_\text{B}T}{h^2} \right)^{3N/2} = \frac{V^N}{N!\Lambda^{3N}}, $$ where $\bar{m}\equiv \left(\prod_{i=1}^{3N} m_i\right)^{\frac{1}{3N}}$ is the geometric average of mass, and $\Lambda\equiv \sqrt{\dfrac{h^2}{2\pi\bar{m}k_\text{B}T}}$ is the thermal de Broglie wavelength.
